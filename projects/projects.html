Sustainable Development Index Prediction Using ​Machine Learning

Random Forest Regressor - Satellite Images

Predicted Sustainable Development Index (SDI) at the municipal level using satellite images such as nighttime lights, ​population, EGDP, urban land percentage, PM2.5, and land temperature. The Bolivian SDG Atlas for the period of 2012 was used as the training and testing data. This dataset comprises 15 development dimensions across 339 municpalities.

By modeling each SDG label we found that satellite data could predict some SDG ​variables effectively. The Best performer was SDG 7: "Affordable and Clean Energy" with a mean R2 of 70%. However, our findings indicate that the models had a dessapointng performance for SDG 15: "Life on Land" and SDG 16: Peace, "Justice and Strong Intitutions". This indicates that although remote sensing data can play an important role in the meassurement and monitoring of the SDGs, For some dimensions of development ground data is still the best indicator

Will A Subway Line Reduce Transit-Related Deaths?

Method: Causal Inference Analysis, DID

The introduction of a subway line did not reduce transit-related ​deaths in the Dominican Republic's capital province. Our ​difference-in-differences (DID) analysis revealed a complex ​relationship: initially, the subway seemed to reduce deaths, but ​later it appeared to increase them. This suggests that the subway ​might contribute to more deaths, likely because more people are ​walking without adequate infrastructure improvements to support ​the increased foot traffic. Further research is needed to confirm ​these findings and to identify necessary infrastructure ​enhancements.

Did the Mass Transportation System in Santo Domingo Improve their Economic Output?

Conducted a Difference in Differences analysis on the causal effect of the introduction of a cable car and a subway line on the economy of the the city of Santo Domingo using Night Time Lights as a proxy for economic activity. Positive and statistically significant causal effect was found indicating that the introduction of these mass transportation systems improved the city's economy.
