Monitoring Sustainable Development Goals Using ​Machine Learning and Satellite Data

Skills: Python, Machine learning, Ridge/Lasso Regression, Remote Sensing

This study investigates the efficacy of remote sensing data, combined with machine learning, as a predictor for development. The analysis considers 15 different development dimensions and a composite Sustainable Development Index (SDI) at the municipal level in Bolivia.

Key findings: This research confirms that satellite data can be a valuable tool for monitoring certain SDG dimensions, offering a scalable approach to development assessment and contributing to more informed policy-making. Specifically I the model predicting SDG 1: No Poverty was the best performing model reaching a 70% R².

Impact and Applications:

This research demonstrated that remote sensing, combined with machine learning, can provide valuable insights to inform policy decisions in data-constrained countries, offering a cost-effective alternative for SDG monitoring in developing countries.

Regional Disparities in SDG Achievement Across Bolivian Municipalities

Skills: Python, Machine learning: Clustering, Agglomeration

To identify and understand regional disparities in Sustainable Development Goal (SDG) achievement, I applied Agglomerative Clustering to group 339 Bolivian municipalities. This analysis categorized them into five distinct regions based on their performance across 15 key development dimensions as well as their geographical location.

Key findings: the analysis highlighted the underperformance of Clusters 2 and 0 in SDG 2 (Zero Hunger) and Cluster 3's struggles with SDG 13 (Climate Action). Furthermore, Cluster 2 consistently lagged across most development dimensions, with achievement below 40% in critical areas such as SDG 1 (No Poverty), SDG 2 (Zero Hunger), SDG 9 (Industry, Innovation, and Infrastructure), and SDG 11 (Sustainable Cities and Communities).

These findings underscore the critical need for region-specific interventions and suggest that development efforts should be strategically concentrated in the municipalities comprising Cluster 2 to significantly improve SDG achievement.

Impact and Applications:

The clustering methodology effectively revealed varying levels of SDG achievement across the identified regions, allowing for a granular understanding of development challenges. By examining these clusters, I was able to pinpoint specific areas needing targeted interventions for policy recommendation.

Georeferencing Special Economic Zones (SEZs) in the Dominican Republic

Skills: Python, Data cleaning, Data manipulation, Data Transformation, dashboards

I developed a dataset that integrates historical administrative data of Special Economic Zones (SEZs) in the Dominican Republic (2000-2016) with their precise geographical coordinates. This linkage enables detailed spatial analysis of the SEZs' impact on local economic development, opening new avenues for studies at municipal and grid levels.

Key Achievement: 74 out of 95 SEZs operational during the period were successfully georeferenced, overcoming the previous limitation of unavailable geographical information for these entities.

Impact and Applications:

This dataset facilitates more granular impact analyses, enabling studies at the municipal level or even at a grid level when combined with other technologies like satellite imagery.

Will A Subway Line Reduce Traffic-Related Deaths?

Skills:

R, Causal Inference Analysis: Difference in Differences

Our analysis revealed a complex relationship between the introduction of the subway and traffic-related deaths. Initially, the subway appeared to reduce fatalities, but later, it seemed to have the opposite effect. This suggests that the subway may have led more people to choose walking or cycling as their primary mode of transportation to access the new infrastructure. However, the lack of adequate complementary infrastructure to support these alternative transportation methods may have contributed to the increased risk.

Strong Regional Dependence of The Dominican Republic's Economic Output

Skills:

Geoda, Spatial Analysis, LISA

Strong and positive spatial dependence between municipalities was observed, indicating that shocks or policies related to economic output introduced in one municipality can influence the economy of neighboring areas. Additionally, a statistically significant cluster of municipalities with high economic activity was identified, including the capital city and its surrounding municipalities. Finally, we concluded that the center of this cluster lies within Distrito Nacional and Santo Domingo Norte, making them key targets for policies aimed at expanding their spillover effects across the region.
