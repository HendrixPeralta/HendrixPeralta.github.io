Monitoring Sustainable Development Goals Using ​Machine Learning and Satellite Data

Ridge Regression - Satellite Images/Remote Sensing

Skills:

Python, Machine learning

I developed Ridge regression models for each SDG to evaluate the predictive power of satellite data. The results revealed that some models exhibited strong predictive performance, with SDG 1: "No Poverty" being the best-performing model, achieving a mean R² of 70%. However, other models performed poorly, particularly for SDG 15: "Life on Land" and SDG 16: "Peace, Justice, and Strong Institutions." These findings suggest that while satellite data can be a valuable tool for monitoring certain SDG dimensions, ground-level data (e.g., surveys) remains essential for tracking and assessing specific aspects of development.

This study predicts the Sustainable Development Index (SDI) at the municipal level using satellite data. We used the Bolivian SDG Atlas 2020, which includes 15 development dimensions across 339 municipalities. The research highlights the potential of remote sensing data in monitoring SDG progress but also emphasizes the need for complementary ground-level data for certain indicators.

Development Challenges of Bolivian Municipalities

Method: Agglomeration Clustering

Skills:

Machine Leaning, Python, Scikit-learn

Using the Agglomeration Clustering machine learning method, we were able to cluster 339 Bolivian municipalities into five regions, taking into consideration the level of achievement of 15 development dimensions. We observed that each region faces different challenges regarding the Sustainable Development Goals (SDG). In this regard, Clusters 2 and 0 are behind the rest of the clusters in terms of SDG2 achievement. In contrast, cluster 3 has an underwhelming achievement rate for SDG13, considering the excellent performance of this cluster in other development dimensions. Particular attention must be drawn to cluster 2, which is consistently behind its peers on most development dimensions except for SDG13.

Will A Subway Line Reduce Traffic-Related Deaths?

Skills:

R, Causal Inference Analysis: Difference in Differences

Our analysis revealed a complex relationship between the subway introduction and Traffic-related deaths: initially, the subway seemed to reduce deaths, but later, it appeared to have the opposite effect. This suggests that the subway might have increased the number of people who chose walking/cycling as a primary method of transportation to use the new subway infrastructure. However, adequate complementary infrastructure has not been improved to support these alternative transportation methods.

Causal Effect of Mass Transportation System on Economic Activity in Santo Domingo

Skills:

R, Difference in Differences

We conducted a Difference in Differences analysis on the causal effect of the introduction of a cable car and a subway line on the economy of the city of Santo Domingo using Nighttime Lights as a proxy for economic activity. A positive and statistically significant causal effect was found, indicating that introducing these mass transportation systems improved the city's economy.

Strong Spatial Dependence of Municipalities's Economic Activity in The Dominican Republic

Skills:

Geoda, Spatial Analysis, LISA

Strong and positive spatial dependence was observed between Municipalities, indicating that shocks/policies introduced in one municipality influence its neighbors. Additionally, a statistically significant cluster of municipalities with high economic activity was found, including the capital city and the municipalities around it. Finally, we concluded that the center of this cluster includes Distrito Nacional and Santo Domingo Norte, making them the target of policies that want to expand their effects across this cluster.
