Monitoring Sustainable Development Goals Using ​Machine Learning and Satellite Data

Ridge Regression - Satellite Images/Remote Sensing

Skills:

Python, Machine learning

This study uses satellite data and machine learning to test the efficacy of remote sensing data as a predictor for development, considering 15 different development dimensions and the composite Sustainable Development Index (SDI) at the municipal level in Bolivia. The results highlight the potential of remote sensing data in monitoring SDG progress but also emphasizes the need for complementary ground-level data for certain indicators.

The analysis revealed that some models like SDGs 7, 9, and 11 exhibited strong predictive performance, with SDG 1: "No Poverty" being the best-performing model. On the other hand other models particularly for SDG 16: "Peace, Justice, and Strong Institutions" performed poorly. These findings suggest that satellite data can be a valuable tool for monitoring certain SDG dimensions. However, ground-level data (e.g., surveys) remains essential for tracking and assessing specific aspects of development.

Development Challenges of Bolivian Municipalities

Method: Agglomeration Clustering

Skills:

Python, Machine learning

To identify these regional disparities, I used Agglomerative Clustering to group 339 Bolivian municipalities into five distinct regions based on their performance across 15 development dimensions. The clustering method revealed varying levels of achievement in the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) across the regions. By examining the resulting clusters, I was able to pinpoint specific challenges for each analytical region, such as the underperformance of Clusters 2 and 0 in SDG 2 and Cluster 3's struggles with SDG 13. These findings underscore the need for region-specific interventions to improve SDG achievement.

Furthermore, cluster 2 consistently lags in most development dimensions when compared to other regions. This suggests that development efforts should be concentrated in the municipalities of cluster 2, especially for SDGs 1: No Poverty, 2: Zero Hunger, 9: Industry, Innovation, and Infrastructure, and 11: Sustainable Cities and Communities, in which this cluster has a percentage achievement　below 40%.

Will A Subway Line Reduce Traffic-Related Deaths?

Skills:

R, Causal Inference Analysis: Difference in Differences

Our analysis revealed a complex relationship between the introduction of the subway and traffic-related deaths. Initially, the subway appeared to reduce fatalities, but later, it seemed to have the opposite effect. This suggests that the subway may have led more people to choose walking or cycling as their primary mode of transportation to access the new infrastructure. However, the lack of adequate complementary infrastructure to support these alternative transportation methods may have contributed to the increased risk.

Strong Regional Dependence of The Dominican Republic's Economic Output

Skills:

Geoda, Spatial Analysis, LISA

Strong and positive spatial dependence between municipalities was observed, indicating that shocks or policies related to economic output introduced in one municipality can influence the economy of neighboring areas. Additionally, a statistically significant cluster of municipalities with high economic activity was identified, including the capital city and its surrounding municipalities. Finally, we concluded that the center of this cluster lies within Distrito Nacional and Santo Domingo Norte, making them key targets for policies aimed at expanding their spillover effects across the region.
