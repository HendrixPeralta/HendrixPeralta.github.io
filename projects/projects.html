Will A Subway Line Reduce Transit-Related Deaths?

Method: Causal Inference Analysis, DID

The introduction of a subway line did not reduce transit-related ​deaths in the Dominican Republic's capital province. Our ​difference-in-differences (DID) analysis revealed a complex ​relationship: initially, the subway seemed to reduce deaths, but ​later it appeared to increase them. This suggests that the subway ​might contribute to more deaths, likely because more people are ​walking without adequate infrastructure improvements to support ​the increased foot traffic. Further research is needed to confirm ​these findings and to identify necessary infrastructure ​enhancements.
