Hello, my name is Hendrix, and I am a master’s student passionate about data science and data analytics. My research interests include machine learning, remote sensing data, regional development, and spatial econometrics.

My experience as a Maintenance Engineer gave me a deep understanding of the value of data-driven decision-making. During this time, I utilized data as a tool to improve the efficiency of maintenance operations by monitoring machine service trends and tracking spare parts usage. This approach enabled us to maintain a lean inventory, optimize maintenance schedules, and minimize disruptions to the production process, ultimately reducing production costs.

Coupled with my interest in data analysis, my experience as a team member in diverse projects made me understand the significance of the economic aspects of these projects. This hands-on experience motivated to deepen my expertise in both topics, and lead me to pursue a master’s in economics and policy management in Japan, where I further developed my analytical and problem-solving skills.

Experience

During my three years as a Maintenance Engineer, I analyzed maintenance services and inventory stock data to guide strategic decisions within the maintenance department. This data-driven approach significantly reduced machine downtime caused by unexpected maintenance events. Additionally, I developed an Excel-based tool that utilized spare parts usage data. This tool enabled the department to keep a lean and efficient inventory system.

Moreover, I strengthened my statistical and analytical skills during my master’s studies. At Nagoya University, I joined a Data Analytics-focused lab. In this lab I worked with causal inference methods like Difference-in-Differences (DID) and Fixed Effects models. Additionally, I used remote sensing data to address the lack of official data availability, allowing me to do an effective analysis of sustainable development in developing countries.

Furthermore, I took advantage of the abundant availability of remote sensing data to apply machine learning clustering and predicting models. This approach allowed me to develop models that effectively gave policymakers a tool to design and monitor development policies at a relatively lower cost when compared to traditional methods.
